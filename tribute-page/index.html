Dorothea Lange

Dorothea Lange was a well-known Great Depression-era photographer best known for her iconic 1936 photograph, Migrant Mother.

Timeline of Dorothea Lange's Life

  • 1895 - born in Hoboken, New Jersey
  • 1902 - contracted polio
  • 1907 - moved to New York City after her father abandoned the family
  • 1918 - plans to travel the world disrupted by being robbed - settled in San Francisco, California
  • 1920 - married painter Maynard Dixon
  • 1925 - first son, Daniel, born
  • 1930 - second son, John, born
  • 1933 - White Angel Breadline photograph captured attention
  • 1935 - divorces Dixon and later married Paul Schuster Taylor
  • 1936 - iconic photograph of Florence Owens Thompson, Migrant Mother was published
  • 1941 - first woman to be awarded the Guggenheim Fellowship in Photography
  • 1945 - began teaching at the California School of Fine Arts (know known as San Francisco Art Institute)
  • 1952 - co-founded the photography magazine Aperture
  • 1965 - died at the age of 70
  • 1984 - inducted into the International Photography Hall of Fame and Museum
  • 2003 - inducted into the National Women's Hall of Fame
  • 2008 - inducted into the California Hall of Fame
